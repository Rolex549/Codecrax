Mental Health

Mental health refers to a person's emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how individuals think, feel, and act, as well as how they handle stress, relate to others, and make choices. Good mental health helps people cope with challenges, maintain relationships, and lead productive lives. Mental health issues, such as anxiety, depression, and stress disorders, are common but treatable. Maintaining mental well-being involves self-care, support from others, healthy lifestyle choices, and, when needed, professional help.

Stress Level

Stress level refers to the degree of mental or emotional strain a person experiences in response to demands or pressures. It can range from low (manageable) to high (overwhelming), affecting physical and mental health. Chronic high stress can lead to issues like anxiety, fatigue, and health problems. Managing stress involves relaxation techniques, time management, exercise, and seeking support when needed.

Body Fitness

Body fitness refers to the overall physical condition of a person, including strength, endurance, flexibility, and body composition. It reflects how well the body performs daily activities and resists disease. Maintaining fitness involves regular exercise, a balanced diet, proper rest, and healthy lifestyle habits.

Exercises

Exercises are physical activities designed to improve or maintain health and fitness. They include aerobic (e.g., running), strength (e.g., weightlifting), flexibility (e.g., stretching), and balance (e.g., yoga) exercises. Regular exercise boosts physical and mental well-being, energy levels, and overall quality of life.
